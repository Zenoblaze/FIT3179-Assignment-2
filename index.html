Esports Across the Globe

Esports refers to "electronic sports". This in particular refers to games of a competitive nature where teams compete to win by defeating other teams of players. Generally tournaments will be held in an elimination or double elimination format similar to chess. There are other styles of tournament, and it depends on the game, and tournament as to how they are run.

Some major esport tournaments for games such as "Dota 2" rival traditional sports in terms of their prize pools with Dota 2's international tournaments reaching prizes of over $40 million USD, that is similar to that of Wimbelton at $45 million USD!

Esports Globally

Esports are not just contained to western countries, with players from all over the world being able to particpate in tournaments online or in person. Although a lot of professional esports players originate from countries such as the USA, China, South Korea, and Russia. It is interesting to note that despite having many casual players, Japan does not have a huge esports presence due to esport events being considered gambling! Esports Teams & Players

Although funding from tournaments may be the most obvious way that professional esport players can earn money, just like more traditional sports there are esport organisation, teams, and players.

Games such as Dota 2, League of Legends, and Fortnite tend to have the biggest prize pools as their parent companies Valve, Riot, and Epic Games are massive and the games themselves have large player-bases. Esports are voliatile in that if the sport loses popularity, the prize pools diminish.

As of 2021 when this data was taken the top 10 games with the highest prize pool are: Dota 2, Counter-Strike: Global Offensive, Fortnite, League of Legends, Overwatch, PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds, StarCraft II, Overwatch, PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds: Mobile, and Hearthstone

There are two main outliers on the graph below. The first being Dota 2. The reason that there are multiple values over $10 million is that every year for their international tournament, sponsored by Valve (a game store platform), a potion of the money earned by Valve via the ingame "battepass" sales is contributed to the prize pool every year.

The other main outlier is one of the fortnite tournaments with an abnormally high value. This tournament was the first Fortnite world cup an international tournament that since 2021 is planned to be annual in a similar manner to Dota 2's international.
